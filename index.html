I am a postdoc in the Statistical Physics and Condensed Matter group at the International Centre for Theoretical Sciences, Bengaluru, India. My current research is on active matter which are systems that are driven out of equilibrium. I am also studying shape transitions in equilibrated elastic surfaces and ribbons.

Previously, as a postdoc in the Cacciuto Group at Columbia University, New York, I studied shape transitions, interactions and work generation in active polymers and active membranes.

As a doctoral student in the Schwarz Group at Syracuse University, New York, I worked on a variety of problems - the compression stiffening in semiflexible networks, nonlinearities in a morphogenesis model of the cerebellum, the correspondence between convexity and rigidity in spring networks under isotropic strain and constructing maximally random null models for mice neural networks using methods of statistical mechanics.
